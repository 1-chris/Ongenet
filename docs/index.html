Sound sources

Instruments

Eight built-in instruments ship in the core. Any CLAP, LV2, VST2 or VST3 instrument you have installed is discovered at runtime and appears right alongside them.

Oscillator

Polyphonic single-oscillator synth — one waveform per voice, shaped by an ADSR envelope.

3× Osc

Triple-oscillator subtractive synth in the spirit of FL Studio's 3x Osc: three oscillators, each with its own waveform, coarse/fine tuning and phase offset.

FM Synth

Two-operator FM — a sine carrier phase-modulated by a sine modulator with adjustable ratio and index, ADSR-shaped.

Basic Sampler

Plays one loaded audio sample pitched across the keyboard (resampled around C4), with an attack/release envelope.

Granular

Continuously spawns short, overlapping windowed grains from a moving playhead over a loaded source sample.

Padda

Lush pad synth — two fat unison layers plus a sine sub and noise through a modulated resonant filter, finished with an internal chorus → delay → reverb chain. Ships an init patch and five presets.

Kicka

Kick-drum synth from clean drumkit thumps to hardstyle screams — a transient "tok" plus a pitch-swept, distortion-stacked tail over a clean parallel sub, with the low end kept mono.

Sampler (SFZ)

Multi-sample SFZ engine — load any .sfz patch and it maps notes and velocities across regions with velocity layers and round-robin, each voice with its own envelopes and filter.

CLAP, LV2, VST2 & VST3 Plugins

Third-party .clap, .lv2, VST2 and .vst3 instruments are scanned at startup and hosted directly — including their native plugin GUIs — with no extra setup.

Workflow

Features

Everything you need to arrange, perform, edit and mix — built on a lock-free audio engine with sample-accurate sequencing.

Timeline & arrangement

A scrolling, zoomable arrange view where MIDI and audio clips live side by side.

  • Drag-to-move, edge-resize and cross-track reparenting of clips, with zoom-adaptive grid snapping (bar / beat / ½ / ¼ / ⅛).
  • Middle-mouse horizontal zoom around the cursor; a moving playhead, start marker and clickable bar ruler.
  • Drop audio files straight from the built-in browser or the OS — WAV decodes natively, other formats transcode via ffmpeg.
  • Configurable arrangement length, time signature and tempo, with live time read-outs.
  • Edit / Select / Slice tools, rubber-band multi-select, duplicate, reverse and slice operations.

Track grouping & mixing

Per-track strips feed group buses and the master, all metered live.

  • Group tracks into bus tracks (group / ungroup / detach) — e.g. fold all your drums into one "Drums" group.
  • Constant-power pan, volume, mute and solo per track, audible in both live play and playback.
  • Live peak metering on every track header plus a stereo master loudness meter.
  • Per-track effect chains, reorderable top-to-bottom, with one-click bypass per effect.

Sidechaining

A dedicated Sidechain effect with two ducking modes, driven by a lock-free cross-track signal bus.

  • Tempo mode — a tempo-synced ducking pump retriggered per note division (1 bar → 1/16, incl. triplets), with adjustable amount, attack and release.
  • Track mode — envelope-follower ducking keyed from any other track or group (so a whole drum group can be the trigger).
  • The same cross-track signal tap powers the Vocoder, whose carrier is read from another track.
  • Fully honoured on offline export, not just live playback.

Stuttero new

A real-time stutter / beat-repeat performance effect in the spirit of Stutter Edit — our own, built right into the effect chain.

  • Captures incoming audio and chops it into tempo-synced slices, from 1/4 all the way down to 1/512.
  • A drawable per-slice gate curve shapes each repeat, routed through a reorderable multi-FX rack — tape-stop, lo-fi, comb, phaser, chorus and low-pass.
  • Gestures bundle the settings plus time-variant curves (stutter-rate sweep, filter cutoff, per-module depth) that you draw and trigger.
  • Fire gestures automatically from the transport, or play them from mapped MIDI keys, with a hold-to-freeze buffer.

Piano roll

A full-featured note editor with its own control bar, music tools and editing modes.

  • Note CRUD with snapping, a "last length" pen, multi-note relative move & resize, and note slicing.
  • Hand-rolled, dependency-free MIDI import & export (Standard MIDI File, round-trip exact).
  • Chord progression generator with diatonic scales and an optional chord-aware melody overlay.
  • Arpeggiator — convert chords into arpeggios across keys and scales.
  • Edit / Select / Slice tools shared with the timeline, live instrument preview while editing, and middle-mouse zoom.

Automation

Automate any knob, slider or switch over time with editable curves.

  • Right-click almost any control to create an automation lane; lanes appear as collapsible rows under their track.
  • Editable curves — add, drag and delete points, bend segments between them, and watch bound controls move live during playback.
  • Record automation in real time by arming a lane and moving the control while the transport rolls.

Recording & performance

Capture MIDI and audio with a familiar arm → count-in → record flow.

  • Per-track arm, a one-bar metronome count-in, and clips that grow live on the timeline as you play.
  • Audio-track input recording with a live-drawing waveform; independent input/output device selection (mono or stereo).
  • Play instruments live from a hardware MIDI controller, the on-screen keyboard, or an FL-style computer-keyboard layout.
  • Optional input quantize snaps recorded notes to the grid (1/4 … 1/32, including triplets).

MIDI controllers

Full hardware MIDI support on every platform, with no extra dependencies — each OS's native MIDI API is driven directly.

  • Cross-platform input — the ALSA sequencer on Linux (works with PipeWire, JACK, USB, software and Bluetooth MIDI), WinMM on Windows, and CoreMIDI on macOS.
  • Play & record — perform the selected instrument live with velocity, and capture into MIDI clips with optional input quantize.
  • MIDI learn — right-click any knob, slider or switch and wiggle a control on your device to bind a CC to it; mappings save with the project and survive undo / redo.
  • Transport & expression — map pads or buttons to play-pause, stop and record; pitch bend, mod wheel, sustain and aftertouch reach the instrument.
  • Device, mappings and live input activity all live in the Settings window; your choice persists to the standard per-OS config location.

Tempo-synced samples

Loops lock to the project grid automatically.

  • Detects a sample's natural BPM from its name/folder or by onset analysis, then time-stretches it to the project tempo with half/double octave snapping.
  • Synced loops re-fit live when you change the project tempo; one-shots and recordings are left at native length.
  • A contextual Sample inspector lets you override the natural BPM and toggle stretching per clip.

Plugins — CLAP, LV2, VST2 & VST3 new

Host your existing plugin collection. Every format is implemented from scratch — direct ABI interop, no wrapper libraries and no Steinberg SDK — and discovered at startup from the standard per-OS locations.

  • CLAP, LV2, VST2 and VST3 instruments appear in the Instruments tab; audio effects slot into any track's effect chain under Plugins.
  • Plugin parameters are exposed as native, automatable controls — and respond to MIDI learn like everything else.
  • Native plugin GUIs open in their own window, embedded for you (on Linux the UI is hosted in a GL-compatible X11 surface so even heavyweight UIs like Cardinal / VCV Rack render correctly).
  • The LV2 host ships a hand-written Turtle/RDF bundle scanner and supports the URID-map, Options and Worker extensions, so sampler- and engine-class plugins load too.
  • The VST3 host speaks the COM-style factory/component/processor/controller model directly — process buses, note & parameter event lists, state transfer and the IPlugView editor — cross-platform on x64 and arm64.
  • Nothing is bundled or required — if you have no plugins installed, Ongenet runs exactly as before.

Themes, history & projects

The conveniences that make a session pleasant to live in.

  • Live theming — 26 semantic tokens, every Catppuccin variant plus light/dark, a built-in theme editor (in the Settings window) and JSON import/export, all applied without a restart.
  • Undo / redo — snapshot-based history with a jump-to-any-point History window (Ctrl+Z / Ctrl+Y).
  • Single-file projects — the .ongen format bundles everything (samples de-duplicated) in one version-tolerant file.
  • Offline export — render the whole arrangement to a WAV faster than real time, sidechain and all.
